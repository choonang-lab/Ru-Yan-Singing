SOVT through the break

Lip-trill, straw, or a closed "voo" siren up and down through your break. The semi-occlusion eases the pressure that triggers gripping — keep one smooth, connected sound across the transition.

"Break detected" means your tone dropped out or dipped sharply crossing the break — continuity is a proxy from pitch + level, not a verdict.

Balanced onsets

Kill the glottal slam. Start each note with a coordinated, cushioned onset — a silent "h" leading the tone, not a punch. Targets sit where your phrases start near a break. Tap a target, play it, then tap Rep and sing one short, clean note.

Onset hardness is estimated from the attack envelope and pitch scoop — a proxy for glottal pressure, not a measurement of your folds.

Breath pacing & sustain

For the long phrases. Find your sustain, then pace it — hold evenly to a target time without the volume tapering early. Even airflow is the goal, not just duration.
